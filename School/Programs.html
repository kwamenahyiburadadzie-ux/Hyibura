Our Academic Programs

1. English Language

Students develop strong communication skills and confidence in public speaking, contributing to Ghana’s reputation for producing excellent speakers and debaters at international competitions like the World Schools Debating Championships.


2. Mathematics

Ghanaian students regularly excel in international math competitions, such as the Pan-African Mathematics Olympiad. The subject also lays the foundation for future engineers, accountants, and tech innovators in Ghana.

3. Science (Integrated Science for Primary, Core Science for JHS)

Basic science education has produced young innovators who have represented Ghana in the Google Science Fair and local STEM competitions. For example, students have created simple water filtration systems and solar-powered devices from recycled materials.

4. Social Studies

Social Studies fosters civic responsibility and national identity. Many students have gone on to become youth parliamentarians and participate in civic engagement programs like the National Commission for Civic Education (NCCE) initiatives.

5. Religious and Moral Education (RME)

Students learn values like tolerance and respect, contributing to Ghana’s high levels of religious tolerance and peaceful coexistence, which is often praised internationally.

6. Creative Arts (Primary) / Career Technology (JHS)

Ghanaian students often showcase their creative talents in visual arts, music, drama, and design. Notably, JHS students participate in district and national Independence Day exhibitions, and some go on to win awards in events like National Science and Maths Quiz design contests and Robofest Ghana.

7. Our World Our People (Primary Only)

This subject promotes environmental awareness. Students often take part in local clean-up campaigns and environmental clubs, contributing to tree-planting drives and sanitation improvement in schools and communities.

8. Ghanaian Language (Fante)

Preservation of indigenous languages and culture. Students become ambassadors of their heritage and participate in national cultural festivals, poetry recitals, and language quizzes promoting local dialects.

9. Computing / ICT

Students gain early exposure to digital skills. Ghana has seen JHS students win coding competitions like Africa Code Week, and some have developed basic mobile apps for their communities.

10. History (JHS)

History education strengthens national identity. Students write essays and participate in debates and quizzes commemorating national events like Independence Day and Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Day, preserving Ghana's heritage.

Lower Primary School (Basic 1-3)

Our primary school program focuses on building strong foundations in literacy, numeracy, and social skills. We use an inquiry-based approach to foster curiosity and creativity in young learners. Core subjects include English, Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies, along with Art, Music, and Physical Education.

Upper Primary School (Basic 4-6)

In middle school, students begin to explore subjects in greater depth while developing independence and critical thinking. Our curriculum includes advanced coursework in Math and Science, along with project-based learning in Humanities and the Arts. Extracurricular opportunities help develop teamwork and leadership skills.

Junior High School (JHS 1-3)

Our high school program prepares students for university and beyond. We offer a rigorous academic curriculum with options for electives, career and college counseling, and standardized test preparation. Students can also engage in internships, community service, and student leadership roles.
